title: 'Sonder'
summary: 'a small reflection on noticing that everyone around me is carrying a full life of their own.'
publishedAt: '2026-06-16T19:38:00'
---

i was out one day, doing nothing particularly special, when i got struck by something i had probably seen a thousand times before.

people were everywhere.

some were laughing. some looked tired. some were talking with so much energy that you could tell the conversation mattered to them. some were quiet, moving through the day like they were carrying something invisible. there were couples, strangers, friends, workers, children, people waiting, people leaving, people arriving.

and for a moment, i really saw them.

not just as people passing by. not as background characters in my own day. real people. each one with a life that feels as central to them as mine feels to me. each one with memories i will never know, worries i will never hear about, dreams that may never cross my path, and small private moments that still mean everything to them.

it reminded me of the word **sonder**.

<Definition
	term='sonder'
	partOfSpeech='noun'
	definition='the realization that every passing stranger is living a life as vivid, complex, and emotionally real as your own.'
/>

i have known the word for a while, but that day it stopped feeling like just a nice word. it became something i could almost feel in the air.

i think it is easy to forget this.

most days, we move through the world from inside our own heads. we think about what we need to do, where we need to be, what someone said to us, what we are worried about, what we are hoping will work out. the world becomes a kind of stage for our own thoughts. other people appear in it, but often only briefly. a cashier, a driver, a person walking too slowly in front of us, someone laughing too loudly, someone taking up space.

but they are not brief to themselves.

their laughter is connected to something. their tears are not random. their silence has a shape. their impatience may have a history. their joy may have taken years to arrive. the person you barely notice might be having one of the best days of their life. the person who seems rude might be trying very hard not to fall apart. the person smiling into their phone might have just received news they will remember for years.

there is something humbling about that.

it makes the world feel bigger, but not in a way that makes me feel small or unimportant. if anything, it makes me feel more connected. it reminds me that i am one life among many lives, one story moving among countless other stories. mine matters, but it is not the only one that matters.

i think that realization can make a person gentler.

not in a dramatic way. more like a small adjustment in how you move through the day. a little more patience. a little more room for people to be complicated. a little less certainty that you know exactly what someone is like from one moment of seeing them.

it also makes ordinary things feel less ordinary.

a group of friends laughing together is not just noise. it is proof of shared history. someone crying quietly is not just a sad image. it is a whole private world spilling out for a moment. a person sitting alone is not necessarily lonely, and a person surrounded by people is not necessarily fine.

there is so much happening beneath what we can see.

that is probably what stayed with me most. how much of life is hidden in plain sight. we are all walking around with years inside us. childhoods, mistakes, names we miss, places we want to return to, things we wish we said differently, people we love, versions of ourselves we are still trying to understand.

and somehow, we meet each other in tiny passing moments.

a glance. a greeting. a small inconvenience. a short conversation. a message. a seat beside someone. most of these moments disappear almost immediately, but sometimes they are enough to remind you that the world is not empty. it is crowded with meaning.

i do not know if this thought will change anything big in me, but i hope it changes small things.

i hope i remember it when i am impatient.

i hope i remember it when i am too quick to judge.

i hope i remember it when i feel like i am the only one carrying something.

because everyone is carrying something. everyone is going somewhere. everyone has a story that makes perfect sense from the inside, even if it is completely invisible from where i stand.

that day, looking at other people, i felt that clearly.

they were real. their laughter meant something. their tears meant something. their lives were happening just as fully as mine.

and for a brief moment, that was enough to make the whole world feel different.
