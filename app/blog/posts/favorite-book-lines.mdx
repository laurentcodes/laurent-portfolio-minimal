i have been reading a little more intentionally lately, and one thing i have noticed is how some lines just stay with you.

sometimes it is because they sound beautiful. sometimes it is because they explain something you have felt for a long time but never really had the words for. sometimes it is just one sentence that makes you pause for a bit before moving on.

so i thought i should keep a few of those lines here. not as a review of the books, because i don't think i can do all of that properly in one post, but more like a small shelf of words i keep coming back to.

### 1984 by George Orwell

- "Freedom is the freedom to say that two plus two make four. If that is granted, all else follows."

this one feels so simple, but that is probably why it hits. it is about truth, and the freedom to say what is obvious even when the world is trying to bend it into something else.

- "If you want to keep a secret you must also hide it from yourself."

this is one of those lines that feels uncomfortable because there is some truth in it. it says a lot about fear, control, survival, and the strange things the mind can do when it is trying to protect itself.

### The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho

- "And, when you want something, all the universe conspires in helping you to achieve it."

i know this line is very popular, but i still like it. there is something hopeful about it. it does not remove the work or the uncertainty, but it makes desire feel less lonely.

### The Seven Spiritual Laws of Success by Deepak Chopra

- "I am that, you are that, all this is that, and that's all there is."

i like how this line makes everything feel connected without trying too hard to explain it.

- "Both past and future are born in the imagination; only the present, which is awareness, is real and eternal."

this one is a reminder i probably need often. i spend a lot of time thinking about what has happened and what could happen, but the present is really the only place anything can be done.

- "This is the genius of nature's intelligence. These are the thoughts of God - the rest are details."

this line feels like a call to pay more attention. sometimes the most important things are already happening quietly around us.

- "Karma is the eternal assertion of human freedom... Our thoughts, our words, and deeds are the threads of the net which we throw around ourselves."

i like this because it puts responsibility back in your hands. what you think, say, and do matters, and over time those things become the life you are standing inside.

### Milk and Honey by Rupi Kaur

- "Losing you  
  was the becoming  
  of myself"

short, painful, and complete. i like lines like this because they do not try to explain too much. they just say the thing and leave you with it.

### The Da Vinci Code by Dan Brown

- "Castigo corpus meum."

this line stayed with me mostly because of how intense the scene around it felt. it is short, but it carries a lot of weight in the story.

### How Not to Die by Michael Greger, MD

- "We cannot let the perfect be the enemy of the good."

this is one of those practical lines i think applies to almost everything. health, work, learning, building, habits, all of it. waiting for perfect can easily become another way to do nothing.

### Think and Grow Rich! by Napoleon Hill

- "You are the Master of Your Fate, the Captain of Your Soul."

i like this because it points back to agency. life will always have things outside your control, but you still have a say in how you move, what you choose, and what you keep becoming.

- "If you pray for a thing, but have fear as you pray, your prayer will have been in vain."

this one is direct in a way i like. it reminds me that wanting something and doubting it at the same time can quietly cancel each other out.

### Closing

i like that these books are all different, but the lines i remember from them still seem to meet somewhere. truth, desire, awareness, responsibility, loss, discipline, health, faith, they all keep pointing back to how a person chooses to live.

that is probably why i like keeping quotes. they are small, but the right one can find you at the right time and stay longer than you expected.
